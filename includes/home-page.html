The Tundra Nunavik project

Climate change and tundra of Nunavik: exposition, sensibility and vulnerability

 Project overview

While most ecological studies focus on the impact of climate change on some populations or species, it becomes clear that ecosystem-based information is needed to better predict the future state of the Arctic. We therefore conducted a large-scale study aimed at characterizing the vulnerability of tundra terrestrial ecosystems to climate change through modelling approach.

Our approach combined climate modeling, climate niche modeling, and ecosystem modeling, to assess potential changes in terrestrial ecosystems of tundra of Nunavik during the 21st century. More specifically we proceeded in a 7-steps approach:

  1. To build the climate of 1981-2010 (baseline period) for the province of Quebec north of 50°N and the Labrabdor at a fine resolution (10km x 10km grid cells)
  2. To project the study area climate for three future periods (2011-2040, 2041-2070 and 2071-2100) under several climate change scenarios
  3. To model the distribution of 183 species (146 birds and 37 mammals) using niche models by relating species distribution to climate conditions
  4. To project species distributions under climate change scenarios during the three future periods (2011-2040, 2041-2070 and 2071-2100) and two RCP (RCP 4.5 and RCP 8.5)
  5. To build trophics networks for the baseline and the three future periods based on niche modeling projections
  6. To quantify potential ecosystem modifications using different metrics (species richness, species gains, species losses, species turnover, network connectance, number of trophic links, etc.)
  7. To develop a vulnerability index to climate for terrestrial ecosystems of the tundra of the Nunavik.
